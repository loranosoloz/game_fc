### DUAL FORMATIONS: IN & OUT OF POSSESSION

Revolutionary Tactical Innovation

The biggest tactical change in Football Manager history. FM26 introduces separate formations for attacking and defending, mirroring how modern football actually works.

#### How It Works

In Possession Formation: Defines how your team positions itself when you have the ball. This is your attacking shape, your creative structure for breaking down opponents. Out of Possession Formation: Shows how your team defends in a settled block, whether pressing high, holding a mid-block, or sitting deep. For example, a team using a 4-3-3 when attacking might shift to a 4-1-4-1 when defending, compressing the midfield to deny space. The transition between these shapes happens organically based on ball possession.

#### Why This Changes Everything

Previously, if you wanted a player to push high as an Attacking Midfielder when attacking, they would also be stuck in that advanced position when defending. Now, that same player can be an AM in possession and drop to a Defensive Midfielder out of possession. This tactical depth allows you to truly replicate the systems of elite managers like Pep Guardiola, Mikel Arteta, and Jürgen Klopp, who constantly shift their team's shape based on possession.

#### Four Tactical Views

The redesigned Tactics menu features four distinct views:

1. In Possession: Your attacking formation and roles
2. Out of Possession: Your defensive formation and roles
3. Both: Side-by-side comparison showing transitions
4. Combined: Average player positions between both formations; your hub for team selection

Any personnel changes made in Combined View automatically apply across both formations.

#### Assistant Manager Support

Worried about the complexity? When you select your In Possession formation, the game suggests three Out of Possession formations that naturally complement your attacking shape. You choose which works best for your system. Your backroom staff will flag if your dual-formation approach carries excessive risk, but ultimately you decide how bold to be.

### COMPLETE PLAYER ROLE OVERHAUL

Modern, Clearer, More Authentic

To support dual formations, FM26 completely overhauls player roles with clearer naming, modern functionality, and separate In Possession and Out of Possession role sets.

#### What's Been Removed

FM26 says goodbye to confusing legacy role names:

- Mezzala - Gone
- Enganche - Gone
- Trequartista - Gone
- Segundo Volante - Gone

Their functionality has been integrated into new, clearly named roles or split between In Possession and Out of Possession variants.

#### Major New In Possession Roles

35+ new roles introduced, including:

- No-Nonsense Goalkeeper - Safety-first approach
- Ball-Playing Goalkeeper - Joins buildup play
- Overlapping Centre-Back - Advances wide to deliver crosses
- Advanced Centre-Back - Transitions into DM during buildup
- Inside Full-Back - Tucks into CB line during buildup
- Pressing Full-Back - Engages high to support press
- Holding Full-Back - Stays deeper while team presses
- Playmaking Wing-Back - Creative hub for attacks
- Advanced Wing-Back - Stays high and wide
- Inside Wing-Back - Moves centrally to support midfield
- Midfield Playmaker - Dictates tempo centrally
- Wide Central Midfielder - Covers wide areas from center
- Box-to-Box Playmaker - Creative engine with energy
- Channel Midfielder - Makes direct runs into channels
- Dropping DM - Drops into backline under pressure
- Screening DM - Blocks central passing lanes
- Pressing DM - Steps forward to press high
- Wide Forward - Striker operating in wide channels
- Channel Forward - Runs between defenders
- Half-Space Forward - Wide attacker drifting inside

#### Entirely New Out of Possession Roles

FM26 introduces a completely new set of roles specifically for defending. These define how players operate when your team doesn't have the ball. A creative Midfield Playmaker in possession might become a Screening CM out of possession (sitting deep to block space) or a Pressing CM (winning the ball high). The same player, two distinct roles based on match phase.

#### Goodbye to Duties

The Attack/Support/Defend duty system has been removed. The flexibility of dual formations and dual roles now gives you a far clearer picture of what your players will do in every phase of play.

#### Greater Customization Freedom

FM26 has stripped back the number of hardcoded instructions tied to each role, giving you more freedom than ever to customize—or even micro-manage—exactly how you want your players to act on Match Day.

### TACTICAL VISUALISER

See Your Tactics Come to Life

The Tactical Visualiser is a powerful new tool that shows exactly how your formations and tactics play out on the pitch. This is game-changing for tactical understanding.

#### How It Works

Found in the Team Instructions menu, the Visualiser divides the pitch into three vertical zones (defensive third, middle third, final third). You can view separate visualizations for both In Possession and Out of Possession. Clicking on any of the nine grid squares reveals how your team's shape shifts as the ball moves across the pitch. Because it's powered by training match scenarios, you see an authentic preview of how your tactical ideas translate in practice.

#### Real Example: Position Changes

Manchester City - Nico O'Reilly as Inside Wing-Back:

- Defensive third (IP): Takes up traditional left-back position
- Middle third (IP): Drifts inside into Defensive Midfield position
- Final third (IP): Continues central movement to support attacks

The Visualiser captures these transitions perfectly, giving you unprecedented insight into player behavior before you even play a match. Brighton - Carlos Baleba as Pressing Defensive Midfielder:

- Defensive third (OOP): Holds usual position at base of midfield
- [*] Pushes forward to actively support the high press

#### Why It Matters

Previously, you could only guess how players would position themselves. Now you see exactly where everyone will be in each pitch zone, allowing you to identify gaps, overlaps, or tactical issues before they cost you points. This tool transforms tactical planning from guesswork to precision.

FM26's reimagined UI is built on three core principles from the Design Team: Efficiency (faster access to information), Familiarity (still feels like FM), and Predictability (easy to learn and master). The result is an interface that's more intuitive, faster to navigate, and provides more contextual information than ever before.

The old-school Inbox has been completely replaced by the Portal - a true home screen combining messages, fixtures, calendar, news, and more in one place.

##### Key Features:

- 4 Message Filters: All, New, Tasks, Unread
- Improved Action Panel: Clearer when decisions are needed
- Advice Dropdown: Quick access to backroom guidance (Coaching, Recruitment, Development, Staffing)
- Integrated Fixtures: Upcoming matches visible at a glance
- Two-Week Calendar Snapshot: Plan training and team selection
- Global News Feed: Relevant stories from around your football world
- Results Hub: Quick access to league standings and match results

The Portal is more than messages—it's your gateway to everything that matters in your save.

#### Tiles and Cards System

Every screen in FM26 uses a unified tile-and-card system:

- Tiles: Provide key snapshots of information at a glance
- Cards: Click any tile to reveal detailed information

This system reduces screen clutter, makes navigation faster, and serves you more contextual data earlier than previous editions. You're more plugged into your club than ever before.

#### Top Navigation Bar

Navigation has moved from the sidebar to the top of the screen with consolidated categories for faster access. Clean, streamlined design with extensive sub-menus when you need to dig deeper.

#### Revolutionary Search

Previously, you could only search for entities (players, clubs, nations). FM26's search now includes:

- Entities: Players, clubs, nations (as before)
- Objects: Contracts, tactics, training schedules
- Information: Statistics, news, reports
- Navigation: Search for any game area like "Training" and get relevant results

Search for any football-related term and you'll see relevant tiles, messages, news items, and guides instantly.

Meet FMPedia, a glossary and user guide brought to life within your career. FMPedia provides explanations for everything from Financial Fair Play and Board Confidence to FM26's new tactical changes. All in a simple, easy-to-use system accessible anytime through either the ribbon icon at the top of the screen or by searching. Perfect for newcomers or managers returning after time away from the dugout. FMPedia helps you learn more about every aspect of the beautiful game without leaving your save.

#### Bookmarks: Customize Your Experience

Bookmarks are quick, direct links to specific parts of your game universe. You start with 6 curated bookmarks based on the most-visited areas of the game. But you have 24 total bookmark options to choose from. Whether your passion is developing Wonderkids, innovating on the tactics board, or dominating the transfer market, bookmarks allow you to customize your managerial world and tailor your game to your priorities. Change your Bookmarks. Change the game.

Across everything in the UI, there's been a focus on raising the bar for accessibility:

- Correcting unreadable font sizes

### RECRUITMENT REVAMP - Powered by TransferRoom

Key improvement

FM26 introduces a comprehensive overhaul of the recruitment system, consolidating scouting and transfers into a single intuitive Recruitment hub within the navigation bar. The new system is powered by a deeper integration with TransferRoom, the real-world online transfer marketplace used by professional clubs worldwide.

#### New Recruitment Hub Features

The Recruitment home screen includes several smart additions to help you prioritize and plan effectively:

- Recruitment Objectives tile - Quick snapshot of board and fan expectations in the transfer market
- Immediate Needs & Squad Planner - Direct link identifying depth issues in your squad
- Contracts Overview - Visual breakdown of contract lengths with automatic highlighting of players with less than 12 months remaining
- Backroom Staff Management - All tools for shaping your backroom team in one place

#### TransferRoom Integration: Requirements

Requirements allow you to communicate directly with every club in your game world, broadcasting your specific transfer needs. When creating an advert, you can specify:

- Position and player roles (both In and Out of Possession)
- Expected playing time (from star player to squad depth)
- Transfer type (permanent or loan)
- Age profile

Your ad circulates to every club, and they respond with suitable players. Your Director of Football makes recommendations, allowing you to scout, approach agents, make offers, or wait for more options.

#### TransferRoom Integration: Pitch Opportunities

The counterpart to Requirements, Pitch Opportunities let you see what other clubs are actively searching for in the transfer market. The TransferRoom Out card displays requirements from other clubs with full details. You can filter by transfer or loan opportunities and directly offer surplus players when you find a match. The Pitch Activity tab tracks everyone you've offered out and any resulting bids. This gives you more autonomy in player sales and makes it easier to find suitable destinations for your players.

#### Smarter Scouting

- Use In and Out of Possession roles in Recruitment Focuses and general scouting searches
- New color-coding functionality to track active Focuses
- Enhanced scout reports that tell you whether a target player naturally fits your current formations

#### Under-the-Hood Improvements

Numerous improvements designed to elevate realism and improve AI behavior:

- Changes to how clubs sign younger players to satisfy Club Visions
- Further tweaks to AI squad building
- FFP having more impact on AI recruitment decisions
- New transfer clauses for individual player awards (Ballon d'Or, Golden Boot, etc.)
- Better logic for preferred positions in loan negotiations
- More loans with future fees and buyback clauses

### FM24 & FM23 SAVE GAME COMPATIBILITY

Continue Your Story

Anyone not ready to step away from their existing save in Football Manager 2024 or Football Manager 2023 can continue that career into FM26 and benefit from this year's new additions.

#### How It Works

When you load FM26 for the first time, you'll see a panel directly above 'Start a New Game' that reads 'Load FM24/FM23 Saved Games.' This panel contains details about how transferring saves works and allows you to begin the process. All relevant new features in FM26 will be present in saves you bring over. In some cases, items or tutorials will trigger immediately after conversion; you'll need to set other things up from scratch (like dual formations for your existing tactics). The panel won't appear again on subsequent launches, but you'll still be able to load old saves. Go into the Load Game menu and follow the instructions for finding and bringing across old saves.
